<ttc_safe> impact — protected vs unprotected questions

For a configurable subset of QA samples we wrap the question in <ttc_safe> tags and prepend it to the context. The compressor leaves protected regions untouched — does this visibly preserve accuracy as aggressiveness rises?

Underpowered result. Every cell scored F1=1.0 on both protected and unprotected (n=3 pairs/cell). On this dataset, compression at every tested aggressiveness preserved QA accuracy — but the sample size is too small to confidently distinguish protected from unprotected. PROTECTED_SUBSET_FRACTION in eval/tasks/qa.py has been raised from 0.2 to 1.0; future runs will test all samples under both conditions.

Latency analysis

p50/p95/p99 across the sweep. E2E (compressed) = TTC compress + LLM call. E2E (baseline) = LLM call alone.

Read these numbers carefully. E2E p95 includes (a) occasional 429-retry tail latency where TTC's 60 req/min server limit pushed individual calls into a backoff window, and (b) client-side rate-limit pacing introduced by --ttc-rpm (default 50/min). The steady-state per-call TTC compress latency is the TTC p50 column — that's the number to compare against TTC's published benchmarks. E2E p95 is the realistic number under our free-tier quota.
